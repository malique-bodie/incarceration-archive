About The Archive

Our Mission
Link to Assignment Write-up
After doing research for a class assignment on transgender women's experiences in prison, I noticed that the data pertaining to said experience was very sparce and ill-connected. The purpose of this archive is to remedy this, by providing a central destination that hosts a wide variety of information on transgender women in prison. It also serves as a signal booster and project funder, with space for visitors of the archive to donate to ongoing research initiatives on incarcerate trans women, therefore fostering a growth of the archive.




Over the course of the semester, we have examined the various conditions women face in prison, and how these conditions are expounded by race and class. The goal of this archive is to expand that analysis to include how the subversion of gender norms defines ones carceral experience.

Often in conversation regarding women in prison, transgender women and gender non conforming folks are disregarded and unacknowledged. This has left a gaping hole in research examining how transgender women's carceral experiences are defined by their identity, and why rates of incarceration are so high in these communities in the first place.

This archive hopes to mend this gap in research, by consolidating the current research in one location, as well as providing a way for people to directly fund research efforts.
